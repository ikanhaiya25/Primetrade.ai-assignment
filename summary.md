##  Methodology

The analysis was conducted by combining historical trader data with Bitcoin market sentiment (Fear/Greed Index) at a daily level.

Key steps included:
- Cleaning raw trade data and converting timestamps into a consistent date format
- Aligning and merging trading data with sentiment data based on date
- Creating key performance and behavioral metrics such as:
  - Daily PnL per trader
  - Win rate
  - Average trade size
  - Trade frequency
  - Long/short ratio
- Segmenting traders into meaningful groups:
  - High vs low risk (based on trade size)
  - Frequent vs infrequent traders
  - Consistent winners vs inconsistent traders

---

##  Key Insights

1. **Higher Profitability During Greed**
   - Traders achieve higher average PnL during Greed periods, indicating stronger and more predictable market trends.

2. **Overtrading in Fear Markets**
   - Fear periods show significantly higher trading activity but lower win rates, suggesting emotionally driven and reactive trading behavior.

3. **Behavioral Shift Based on Sentiment**
   - Traders adjust their behavior depending on market sentiment:
     - Increased trade frequency during Fear
     - More selective and efficient trading during Greed

4. **Risk Exposure Patterns**
   - Larger trades (higher risk) tend to perform better in Greed markets but underperform in Fear conditions.

---

##  Strategy Recommendations

### 1. Risk Management in Fear Markets
- Reduce trade frequency to avoid overtrading  
- Use smaller position sizes to limit losses  
- Focus on capital preservation rather than aggressive returns  

### 2. Optimized Trading in Greed Markets
- Focus on fewer, high-quality trades  
- Apply trend-following strategies  
- Increase position size moderately while controlling leverage  

---

##  Conclusion

Market sentiment has a strong influence on both trader behavior and performance. By adapting trading strategies based on sentiment conditions, traders can improve profitability while managing risk more effectively.
